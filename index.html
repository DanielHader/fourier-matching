Fourier Matching

This interactive demo lets you play around with Fourier series parameters. The Fourier series is defined as a big sum of periodic functions, typically sine or cosine waves. Each periodic function in the sum has a different integer period and, surprisingly, with enough different periods and carefully chosen amplitudes and phase shifts, any periodic function can be approximated. The form of the Fourier series used in this demo is \[\sum_{n=0}^N A_n\cos\big(2\pi(nx - p_n)\big)\] where \(A_n\) is the \(n\)th amplitude and \(p_n\) the \(n\)th phase. Play around with the various amplitudes and phases and see what shapes you can make.
